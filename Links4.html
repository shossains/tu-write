Links for further reading

On Paragraphs

Purdue Online Writing Lab discusses the four elements that each paragraph should contain: unity, coherence, a topic sentence and adequate development. The section on Paragraphing discusses how to create well-balanced paragraphs. The idea is to choose a paragraph length that is proportional to the length of the paper. Shorter papers have shorter paragraphs.

https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/academic_writing/paragraphs_and_paragraphing/index.html

Paragraphs

The Writing Center of Walden University offers an extensive online course. The paragraph is treated as a measure of thought, each paragraph advancing a claim that builds on the claims of the preceding paragraphs. For organizing paragraphs they recommend to follow the MEAL Plan: Main idea, Evidence, Analysis, Lead out.

http://academicguides.waldenu.edu/writingcenter/paragraphs/home

Logische indeling alinea’s

(This website is in Dutch.)

Deze website behandelt twee soorten alinea’s: de structurerende en de thematische alinea. Structurerende alinea’s worden veel gebruikt aan het begin en aan het eind van een stuk tekst.

https://www.scribbr.nl/nederlandse-taal/logische-indeling/

Looking for a signpost?

When you are looking for the correct signpoast, the Manchester Phrasebank may help you out. Here, you will find several options to explain causes and effects. 

http://www.phrasebank.manchester.ac.uk/explaining-cause-and-effect/
