Referencing using author/year style

Characteristics of the author/year style:

  • This style is often used in the humanities.
  • In-text citations give the name of the author and the year of publication to refer to a reference. Add the page number(s) when quoting a source.
  • The list of references at the end of the text is alphabetised on the last name of the author.
  • As a reader, if you are interested to see which exact publication the author of the text refers to, you simply look up the name of the author in the alphabetised reference list.
  • Examples: American Psychological Association (APA) and Harvard.
