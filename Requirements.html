Guidelines for the title page

The title page is a very important part of your report. On the title page your reader will find information about the contents and the context of your report. Which elements are to be found on the title page? 

The title page must have:

  • A title and subtitle that convey the content of the report and its purpose.
  • The name(s) of the author(s) (make sure it stands out).
  • The date (and place) to state when and where the report was published.
  • The name(s) of the customer(s) or lecturer(s) who assigned the task.
  • The company logo if the author works with a company.

The title page can have:

  • An appealing image that is somewhat relevant to the subject.
  • Course information (in an educational context), like course name or number, faculty and university.

The title page will not have:

  • A page number, start numbering after the title page.
  • Misspelled names of lecturers or companies.
