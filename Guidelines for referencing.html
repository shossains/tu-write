What are references and why are they used?

References can be compared to the end credits after a movie: everyone who has helped to make the movie is mentioned. In academic writing, it is not only a matter of politeness to show whose ideas were used, it is also a necessary requirement to avoid accusations of plagiarism. You have to give credits to whom they are due.

Whenever you use other researchers’ results, ideas, illustrations or data, you have to show whose work you have used. Whenever you summarize, paraphrase, or quote from another source, you have to include an in-text citation in the text that you are writing and take up a complete title description of the work that you referred to in a reference list. Every in-text citation in your paper must have a corresponding entry in your reference list.
