Guidelines for the table of contents

Structuring a table of contents can be quite a puzzle. You need to put all steps in your story in a logical order. Any flaw in the structure of your report will immediately stand out in the table of contents. That is why supervisors and communication skills teachers will often pay close attention to the table of contents.

Besides aiding your reader with finding information, the table of contents gives an outline of the full report. In order for this to happen, the structure must be logical and sections must have informative titles.

Informative

As you have seen in the video, the table of contents should contain all standard report elements: all items in the front matter, all chapters and sections, and all items in the back matter.

Also, the topic of a report must be clear from the table of contents alone. The table of contents outlines the storyline of the report by using informative titles.
