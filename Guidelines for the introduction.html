Guidelines for the introduction

This learning block is about a very important part of your report: the introduction. Next to the summary, the introduction is the part of a report that is read most often.

An introduction has three parts. First, you address the reason for your research. You introduce your topic and you explain why the research was done. Next, you tell about the aim of the research. Finally, you describe the structure of the report.

It is best to address each part in a paragraph group, separated with an open line. A construction like this with a fixed order will assure that your reader can easily find the information he expects.
