What to put in an appendix?

Your body text should contain the most important data and results of your research. Use these data and results to make your point and make sure the information is relevant for all readers. All other data and results should be put in appendices. These data and results are not relevant for all readers.

Check whether the body text can be fully understood without the appendices. A graph or table that you discuss in the body, should also be in the body, otherwise your reader would have to leaf to the appendices and back to the text.

Here is a list of content that is best put in an appendix:

  • detailed calculations
  • extensive tables
  • diagrams of the construction of a device
  • the explanation of a mathematical formula, unless your topic is the formula
  • the survey form you have used to collect your data
  • all relevant tables, figures and analyses you made but are not necessary to understand your body text
