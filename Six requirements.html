Seven guidelines for the conclusion

The conclusion is one of the most often read parts of a report. What does a reader expect to find in the conclusion?

After having read the introduction, some readers immediately look for the conclusion. There they expect to find the answer to the question that was in the introduction.

Other readers will read everything from cover to cover. Finally, they arrive at the conclusion. Will they still remember the question from the introduction?

It is a good thing to start your conclusion by repeating the exact same question from the introduction. Therefore, the first guideline is: restating and answering the main question.
