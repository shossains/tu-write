Layout techniques for quick access

Some layout techniques ensure quick access to a text.

Titles and headings

  • Titles and headings should be easily distinguishable. Use white lines, larger font, bold, italic or underline.
  • Consider using different formats for chapter titles, section titles, subsection titles and headings. However, be consistent in the format that you use for titles on the same level of the text.
  • Beware: titles and headings are not considered to be a part of the text. Professional writers know that the main editor may sometimes delete headings, for example because the layout does not fit well on the page. This means that the text should still be fully comprehensible, even if the heading is deleted. 

When writing in a group, agree upon the formats that you will use. This may save much time when you need to proofread and edit.
