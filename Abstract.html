The art of abstract writing

Summaries can be found in reports, whereas abstracts are usually found in scientific papers. Note that an abstract is a summary, but a summary is not an abstract. Find out about similarities and differences. 

Differences

  • Summaries are short, but abstracts are even shorter. Regarding the latter, usually a single lean paragraph will do, whereas a summary often takes one A4.
  • Abstracts never have figures or tables. In a summary important figures or tables may be added.
  • Summaries are never paired with keywords. Abstracts often are.

Similarities

  • Abstract and summary are both written in complete sentences.

  • Abstract and summary are both written in the present simple and the past simple, because they answer four questions:

    • What is it about? – Present simple
    • What did you do? – Past simple
    • What did you find? – Past simple
    • What does it mean? – Present simple
  • Abstract and summary both include definitions of technical abbreviations. However, as the audience of a science paper mostly consists of specialists, you need not explain what is obvious to them.
